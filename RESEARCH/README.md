# Research

This directory contains research notes on existing international frameworks, declarations, constitutional experiments, legal principles, and ethical foundations relevant to the development of a Constitution of a Living World.

The purpose of this research is not to replace or compete with existing documents.

Instead, it seeks to understand:

* What humanity has already learned.
* Which principles have proven valuable.
* Which ideas remain incomplete.
* Where gaps still exist.
* Whether a shared framework of responsibility toward life, nature, and future generations can be articulated more clearly.

The documents collected here represent different approaches to global responsibility:

| Document                                 | Primary Focus                                              |
| ---------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| Earth Charter                            | Ethical foundations and the value of life                  |
| Global Pact for the Environment          | Environmental rights and legal responsibility              |
| Constitution for the Federation of Earth | Global governance and accountability                       |
| Rights of Nature                         | Nature as a subject of rights                              |
| Future Generations                       | Intergenerational justice and long-term responsibility     |
| Pact for the Future                      | Contemporary international consensus and global challenges |

## Research Principles

This project approaches all source materials with curiosity rather than ideology.

The goal is not to prove a predetermined conclusion.

The goal is to learn from existing ideas, identify their strengths and limitations, and explore whether they can contribute to a broader framework centered on responsibility, life, and the long-term consequences of human actions.

## Core Question

Can civilization be built not only on rights and freedoms, but also on responsibility for the traces we leave behind?

## Methodology

Each research document contains:

- A summary of the source.
- Key ideas and principles.
- Strengths and limitations.
- Lessons relevant to a Constitution of a Living World.

The goal is not to reproduce these documents, but to understand what they contribute and where unanswered questions remain.
